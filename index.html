Notes from Oxford English File Advanced Student's Book and EOI Santa Coloma classrooms.

Generic pronouns

  1. YOU: people in general
    You can learn a language faster if you got to live ...
  2. ONE + 3rd person singular: more formal than you
    One     tends to have problems understanding very strong accents
    Possessive adjective: One's first reaction is ...
  3. WE: opinion which includes the listener or speaker
    When we talk about an accent, we must not confuse this pronunciation
  4. THEY: talk about other people (informal), or people in authority
    They always say... (people in general)
    They should make it compulsory... (the government)

Reflexive and reciprocal pronouns

When subject and object are the same person:

  • You need to look after yourself with that cold.
  • He's very egocentric. He always talks about himself

We don't use reflexive pronouns with some verbs which may be reflexive in other languages: wash, shave, ...

Enjoy is always used with reflexive pronoun: Enjoy yourself

When after most prepositions if the complement is the same that the subject:

  • I was really pleased with myself

(but not after prepositions of place: He put the bag next to her NOT next to herself)

Another cases:

  1. to emphasize the subject: We decorated the house ourselves
  2. by + reflexive pronoun = alone, on your/her/... , own
    I don't feel very comfortable going to the cinema by myself
  3. each other / one another for reciprocal actions (entre nosaltres / entre ells)
    My ex-husband and I don't talk to each other any more.
    My mother and sister don't understand one another at all

It and there

Example sentences:

  • A lack of sleep produces stress
  • A lack of income leads to a job searching (most of people to search/searching a job
  • A lack of motivation contributes to depression
  • A worker underpaid may cause discouragement

  • Stress arises from a lack of sleep

to + infinitive

List of verbs we usually use:

advise, allow, ask, beg (pregar,suplicar), cause, enable, encourage, expect, force, help, intend, invite, mean, order, persuade, recommend, remind, take (time), teach, warn

  1. She advise him not to travel by train
  2. We expect the flight to arrive at 19.50
  3. I took us ages to get there
  • After advise, persuade, remind, teach, tell, warn you can: that + clause
    Ex: He advise me that I should stay off work for two weeks
  • After recommend:
    He recommend me to take some cash
    He recommend that I took some cash
  • Without subject and advise, allow, recommend, encourage: GERUND
    We don't allow eating and drinking on the premises
    I recommend visiting the castle

for + object + to + infinitive

arrange for : We've arranged for a taxi to come at 6.30

ask for : The policeman asked for my license to verify my identity

plan for : I've planned for a holidays to go to London

wait for : I'm waiting for my friend to arrive

more

want, would like, would love, would prefer, would hate + object + to + infinitive

  • I want Arsenal to win
  • I would hate you to think that I don't appreciate your offer of help
  • I'd like you to send me the bill

verb + object + gerund

We often use the following verbs:

dislike, hate, imagine, involve, keep, mind, prevent, remember, risk, stop

  • Please don't keep me waiting!
  • I dislike people telling me what to do
  • I don't mind you smoking in the garden

Passive

Gerunds: being done

Infinitives: to be done

Describes actions which are done to the subject

She hates being told she should do more exercise.

Action: do more exercise

Subject: She

My car needs to be serviced

Perfect

Gerunds: having done

infinitive: to have done

Emphasize that an action is completed or in the past

He thanked them for having helped him

How wonderful to have finished all our exams

Simple and perfect (same meaning):

Gerund:

He denied stealing the money (simple gerund)

He denied having stolen the money (perfect gerund)

Infinitive:

We were silly not to lock the car (simple infinitive)

We were silly not to have lock the car (perfect infinitive)

Perfect infinitive after:

would like / love / hate / prefer / rather

I would like to see the Eiffel Tower (earlier future)

I would like to have seen the Eiffel Tower (earlier past)

Continuous infinitive

To say that action or event is in progress around the time we are talking about.

to be + verb + ing

I'd like to be lying on the beach right now.

She seems to be coughing a lot (sembla que està tossint molt)

Extra

gerund is noun

Different meanings gerund or Infinitives

(for ever)

stop buying bread (que no compri més pà)

stop smoking is good for your health (deixar de fumar per sempre)

(interrupt an action)

stop to buy bread (que pari un moment, interromp l'acció de comprar pà)

stop to smoke a moment (para una moment de fumar...)

Otherwise, there is some verbs with the same meaning:

start to ...

start verb+ing
